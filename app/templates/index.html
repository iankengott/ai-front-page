About Us

Annually 1.35 million people fall victim to fatal car accidents, one of the leading reasons behind the high mortality rate of car crashes is the time it takes for an ambulance to be dispatched and stabalize the patient. The difference between 7 and 12 minutes can make as much as a ~45% difference in mortality rate. One of the best methods to increase car crash survivability would be to reduce ambulance dispatch time. Through meticulous hours of processing over 18 thousand images and weeks of work cumulated we have arrived at (name). (Name) uses Yolov5, an object detection AI in order to accurately spot vehicles that have been posed in both minor accidents and mortal danger in real time utilizing street cam footage and is capable of forwarding it to the correct outlets to hasten ambulance response times to make the difference.

How It Works

Our AI utilizes yolov5 an AI object recognition model in order to facilitate operations and was trained on 2 separate models for crash and vehicle detection, the first model comprising of a collection of crashes with differing levels of severity in a dataset of over ~1400 images, and the second utilizing ~13,000 images to complete its dataset and consisting of cars/motorcycles for vehicle recognition. In order to iron out some flaws inside of our AI we had trained it on several different occasions on both local and cloud runtimes with enough epochs that it had stopped itself. With such a constitution (name) can perform as optimally as possible.

Real world intergration

In a setting where minutes matter, car crashes need to be responded to as quickly as possible. The amount of time it takes for a crash to be reported and an ambulance to be dispatched can take as little as a few seconds North to 10 minutes, the major determining factor being whether you live inside of an urban or rural area. Traffic cameras are stationed all across the United States both urban and rural, with the application of AI we hold the potential to dramatically reduce the staggering 1.35 million annual casualties to car crashes.
